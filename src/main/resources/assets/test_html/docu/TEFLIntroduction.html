Why Should You Get Certified?

A TEFL (Teaching English as a Foreign Language) certificate can give you a major competitive advantage when it comes to finding an ESL teaching job. Being a native English speaker certainly means you know how to communicate in your language, but it doesn’t necessarily mean you know how to explain the “why’s” of your language.

TEFL certification proves that you know how to explain the inevitable questions of English learners, particularly when it comes to tricky grammar and nonsensical pronunciation.

How to Pick the Right Course:

In order to determine what course you should take, you need to consider a few points:

  1. Time

    Two options: onsite intensive, or online self-paced. We recommend onsite courses that include a practicum (see #5).

  2. Location

    You can take it in your home country or attend a program in your target country (this can often be cheaper in the long run as program costs abroad are often lower).

  3. Budget

    Most high-quality and reputable programs cost around $2,000 USD. Although a big investment, keep in mind you get what you pay for.

  4. Quality

    Make sure you take an accredited course that is recognized world-wide. This can often determine the salary an employer is willing to offer, and you can check out the most reputable courses in the next section.

  5. Practice

    The advantages of having an on-site course is that you get real-life practice at applying the teaching concepts you have been learning. This is invaluable as studying from a textbook can never compare to managing a full classroom of students.
